## 1. Project Summary

Project Atlas is the internal codename for Harshitha Devineni’s personal portfolio and digital identity system.

This is not a traditional portfolio website.

This is a living, interactive, AI-enabled thinking space designed to help visitors experience what it feels like to think alongside Harshitha.

The website should make the visitor feel:

* inspired
* deeply curious
* personally connected
* confident that Harshitha is someone they want on their side
* convinced that she can thoughtfully figure things out, even when the problem is messy or unfamiliar

The core impression should be:

> She turns messy ideas into elegant systems, and she is exactly the kind of person I need in the room.

---

## 2. Core Positioning

Harshitha is not positioned as only an engineer, strategist, product thinker, student, consultant, or AI enthusiast.

She is positioned as a thoughtful operator who bridges:

* technology
* business
* people

## 9. Harshitha’s Thinking Style

When given a messy problem, Harshitha naturally asks:

* What is the core problem?
* Is there an underlying problem that is not apparent?
* Are we looking at this through the right lens?
* Are there other ways to frame this?
* Who is affected?
* What does the customer actually experience?
* What does success really look like?
* Why does this matter?
* Why?
* Why?
* Why?

She thinks in multiple modes:

* verbally
* visually
* through lists
* through stories
* through systems
* through categories

She often works by:

1. understanding the messy whole
2. breaking it into simpler problems
3. making each smaller answer feel obvious
4. building back up into a thoughtful solution

Her strongest intellectual trait is empathy.

Her problem-solving philosophy:

> You are not designing for the problem you personally perceive. You are designing for the person living inside the problem.

## 14. Ask Harshitha AI

Ask Harshitha AI is represented by an orb.

The orb should be visually present, inviting, and gently alive.

It should not be annoying.

It should act like curiosity.

Behavior:

* visible as a subtle floating orb
* clickable
* professional and curious in tone
* may use a light pun when the moment naturally fits
* introduces itself when opened
* should be grounded in Harshitha’s actual content
* should admit uncertainty when needed

Possible introduction:

> Hi, I’m Ask Harshitha. I’m a retrieval-augmented guide trained on Harshitha’s work, notes, projects, and thinking system. Ask me anything about how she thinks, what she has built, or where to start.

If uncertain, the AI may say:

> I’m not fully sure based on what I know. Would you like to contact Harshitha directly?

The AI should not fake knowledge.

Initial AI scope:

* answer questions about Harshitha
* guide users through the site
* explain projects
* surface relevant experiences
* summarize her thinking style
* answer recruiter-style questions based on available content

Later AI scope:

* recruiter mode
* job description upload
* personalized portfolio paths
* custom PDF generation
* voice tour
* returning visitor memory
* adaptive recommendations

## 15. Recruiter Mode

Recruiter Mode is a major future feature.

Purpose:

Allow a recruiter or hiring manager to upload or paste a job description and receive a tailored explanation of how Harshitha’s background aligns with the role.

Recruiter Mode should:

* analyze the JD
* identify key capabilities required
* map relevant experiences
* surface relevant projects
* highlight skills demonstrated
* cite project pages or Thinking Dossiers
* avoid exaggeration
* remain professional and grounded
* never claim skills not evidenced in the knowledge base

The intended effect:

> This is exactly who we need.

Recruiter Mode should not feel like keyword stuffing.
It should feel like strategic alignment.

## 30. The Rules of Harshitha

These rules guide every design and product decision.

1. Every project starts with a question, not a solution.
2. Every claim should be backed by evidence.
3. Every interaction should reveal character.
4. Every animation must communicate meaning.
5. The website should invite thinking, not demand attention.
6. AI augments curiosity, never replaces it.
7. The desk is not decoration. It is the heart of the experience.
8. The library is not a reading list. It is evidence of evolving thought.
9. Show the thinking, not just the outcome.
10. Reward exploration, not speed.
11. Beautiful is meaningless without clarity.
12. Every page should answer “So what?”
13. Avoid overconfidence. Communicate grounded confidence.
14. Empathy is a strategic capability.
15. Failure belongs in the story when it teaches something.
16. The visitor should feel invited, not impressed at.
17. If something exists only because it looks cool, remove it.
18. The portfolio should evolve like software.
19. The website should feel like Harshitha, not like a template.
20. The final question is always: does this help someone understand what it feels like to collaborate with Harshitha?

She is someone they would want beside them when the answer is unclear, the problem is messy, and the stakes matter.
